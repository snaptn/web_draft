Development Rubric

This rubric is used to evaluate proposed projects in relation to Wedgewood Houston's community needs and wishes. Projects must meet at least a minimum score of 8 to be eligible for Wedgewood Houston community/SNAP support (letters, etc.)

Category Strong (2 Points) Sufficient (1 Point) Lacking (0 Points)
Community Character
Example: reuses exist. Railyard-scale reuse. Art & utility visible, etc.
 Design and development plan are respectful of community character and history. Proposed buildings symbolize a reimagining of historical neighborhood structures. Unit makeup reflects surrounding community needs. Proposed development includes units that are different from the character of neighborhood and surrounding properties. Size of proposed units do not fit the scale of the need in the community. Building stands out from community character. No focus on maintaining historical character or meeting the community's needs.
Housing Diversity
Examples: various unit formats, affordable income (AMI) definitions, rental units, family focused.
 At least 30% of proposed housing/unit options are diverse housing options. Only one type of diverse housing/units included in plan, BUT still represents at least 10% of the proposed units within the project. Less than 10% diverse housing/units, included in the proposed project.
Transportation Consideration
Examples: parking, walkability, sidewalks, etc.
 Clear design for access to public and personal transportation needs. Improvements in neighborhood walkability, including pedestrian safety. Plan minimally addresses pedestrian safety around busy streets, near schools, etc. Vague language surrounding developer's commitment to public or personal transportation needs. No consideration of public or personal transportation needs or pedestrian safety.
Sustainability
LEED = Leadership in Energy & Environmental Design (examples: water efficiency, renewable materials, structural design efficiency, etc.)
 Plan for EITHER 10% increase in trees or unique use(s) of green space with focus on mitigating urban runoff. Inclusive, welcoming public spaces open to neighborhood and community members. Multiple LEED concepts firmly established. Meets more than the minimum required open space, storm water, and landscape requirements, as defined in the Metro Nashville Zoning Ordinance. Plan for inclusive and welcoming open space. Some LEED concepts established. Meets ONLY the minimum required open space, storm water, and landscape requirements, as defined in the Metro Nashville Zoning Ordinance. Lacks plan for inclusive and welcoming public open space. No LEED concepts established.
Artist/Creative
Could be viewed within Housing Diversity category.
 Proposed project focuses on inclusion of affordable maker space(s), entrepreneurial education, STEM, industrial space or art spaces that are inclusive in design and meant to welcome all neighbors. Includes potential outreach plan, to recruit diverse array of local artists, for onsite installations. Focus on limited types of artistic/creative space. Lacks plan to ensure equity in either types of projects or artists inclusion. No recruitment plan to ensure inclusive diversity. No focus on artistic/creative space.

Community Priorities

SNAP evaluates proposed development projects within the WeHo community using a rubric that was formed with community input to ensure development projects address priorities and preserve the history of the neighborhood.

Developers should incorporate this rubric into presentations when meeting with the community.

SNAP members will score proposed development projects using the rubric to determine if neighborhood support is present.

Note: This rubric is a living document and may change to fit the changing needs of the Wedgewood Houston Community.
